Fingerprinting Smartphones via Embedded Sensors

Project Overview

The objective of this project is to determine the feasibility of fingerprinting smartphones using embedded motion sensors under real-world constraints and on a large number of smartphones. We also explore the development of simple, yet effective countermeasures against such sensor fingerprinting techniques that can attain an acceptable tradeoff between utility and privacy.

Publication:

The paper has been accepted for publication at PoPETs 2018.

Datasets:

We have made our datasets public for the resarch community.
  • Dataset where phones were kept on top of a table [Ondesk_dataset]
  • Dataset where phones were held in the hand of users while sitting down [Inhand_dataset]

Usability study:

The Labyrinth browser-based game used for analyzing the usability of our proposed countermeasures is also available online.
